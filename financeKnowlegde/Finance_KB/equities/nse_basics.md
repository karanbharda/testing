# NSE Basics

## National Stock Exchange (NSE)

The National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) is India's largest stock exchange by market capitalization and trading volume. Established in 1992, it operates under the regulatory framework of the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI).

## Market Segments

### Cash Market (Equity)
- Trading in equity shares of listed companies
- Delivery-based settlement (T+2)
- Price bands: ±10% for most stocks, ±5% for select stocks
- Circuit breakers: 10%, 15%, 20% based on market movement

### Derivatives Market
- Index futures and options on Nifty 50, Bank Nifty
- Stock futures and options on eligible stocks
- Currency derivatives (USD-INR)
- Commodity derivatives

## Trading Hours
- Monday to Friday: 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM IST
- Pre-open session: 9:00 AM to 9:15 AM
- Post-close session: Limited trading

## Order Types
- Market Order: Execute at best available price
- Limit Order: Execute at specified price or better
- Stop Loss Order: Convert to market order when trigger price reached
- Good Till Cancelled (GTC): Valid until cancelled or expiry

## Circuit Breakers
- Level 1: ±10% movement → 15-minute cooling period
- Level 2: ±15% movement → 15-minute cooling period
- Level 3: ±20% movement → Trading halted for the day

## Settlement Cycle
- Rolling settlement with T+2 payout
- Pay-in: T+1 by 11:00 AM
- Pay-out: T+2 by 1:00 PM
- Securities: Dematerialized form mandatory

## Risk Management
- Position limits for derivatives
- Margin requirements (SPAN, Exposure margins)
- Daily settlement and mark-to-market
- Surveillance and monitoring systems
