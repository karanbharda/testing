# Fundamental Analysis & Financial Statement Analysis

## Financial Statement Structure

### Balance Sheet (Statement of Financial Position)

**Assets:**
1. **Current Assets**
   - Cash and cash equivalents: Liquid funds
   - Trade receivables: Money owed by customers
   - Inventory: Raw materials, WIP, finished goods
   - Current investments: Short-term securities
   - Prepaid expenses: Paid in advance

2. **Non-Current Assets**
   - Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E): Tangible assets
   - Intangible assets: Patents, trademarks, goodwill
   - Long-term investments: Strategic holdings
   - Deferred tax assets: Tax benefits

**Liabilities:**
1. **Current Liabilities**
   - Trade payables: Money owed to suppliers
   - Short-term borrowings: Debt due within 12 months
   - Current maturities: Long-term debt due soon
   - Accrued expenses: Costs incurred but not paid

2. **Non-Current Liabilities**
   - Long-term borrowings: Debt due > 12 months
   - Deferred tax liabilities: Future tax obligations
   - Provisions: Estimated future obligations

**Equity:**
- Share capital: Nominal value of shares
- Reserves: Retained earnings, revaluation reserves
- Surplus: Profits retained in business

### Profit & Loss Statement (Income Statement)

**Revenue Section:**
- Revenue from operations: Primary business income
- Other income: Interest, dividends, rental income
- Less: Cost of materials: Direct product costs
- Less: Employee benefits: Salaries, benefits

**Operating Profit:**
- Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation, Amortization (EBITDA)
- EBITDA Margin: % of revenue (higher is better)
- Operating Leverage: Impact of fixed costs

**Profit Levels:**
1. **EBITDA**: Operational efficiency
2. **EBIT**: Operating profit after depreciation
3. **Profit Before Tax (PBT)**: Before taxes
4. **Profit After Tax (PAT)**: Net profit
5. **Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: Profit per share

### Cash Flow Statement

**Operating Activities:**
- Cash from operations: Core business cash generation
- Changes in working capital: Impact on cash
- Adjustment for non-cash items: D&A, stock options
- Shows true cash position

**Investing Activities:**
- Capital expenditure (CapEx): Investment in assets
- Investment in securities: Expansion funding
- Sale of assets: Cash realization

**Financing Activities:**
- Borrowings/repayment: Debt changes
- Equity raises/buybacks: Shareholder structure changes
- Dividend payments: Cash returned to shareholders

## Key Financial Ratios

### Profitability Ratios

**Gross Profit Margin:**
- Gross Profit / Revenue
- Indicates pricing power and production efficiency
- Higher margin: Better unit economics
- Industry specific: Varies by sector

**Operating Profit Margin:**
- Operating Profit / Revenue
- Shows operational efficiency
- Unaffected by financing decisions
- Comparable across companies

**Net Profit Margin:**
- Net Profit / Revenue
- Overall profitability after all expenses
- Varies widely by industry
- Trend more important than absolute value

**Return on Assets (ROA):**
- Net Profit / Average Total Assets
- How efficiently assets generate profit
- Low ROA: Assets underutilized
- High ROA: Efficient management

**Return on Equity (ROE):**
- Net Profit / Average Shareholder Equity
- Return generated for shareholders
- >15%: Good performance
- <5%: Concerning performance
- Subject to leverage effects

### Liquidity Ratios

**Current Ratio:**
- Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- Ability to meet short-term obligations
- Ideal range: 1.5 - 2.5 (industry dependent)
- >3: May indicate unused capital
- <1: Solvency concern

**Quick Ratio (Acid Test):**
- (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
- More stringent than current ratio
- Inventory excluded (harder to liquidate)
- Should be >1: Good short-term health
- Below 1: May need to sell inventory

**Cash Ratio:**
- Cash & Equivalents / Current Liabilities
- Most conservative liquidity measure
- 0.5+: Good cash position
- Highest priority claims (preferred)

### Solvency/Leverage Ratios

**Debt-to-Equity Ratio:**
- Total Debt / Shareholder Equity
- Capital structure indicator
- Higher: More leverage, higher risk
- Industry specific: Varies significantly
- Banks: Can sustain 5-10x leverage

**Debt-to-Assets Ratio:**
- Total Debt / Total Assets
- % of assets financed by debt
- Below 50%: Healthy
- Above 60%: High leverage risk

**Interest Coverage Ratio:**
- EBIT / Interest Expense
- Ability to service debt
- >3: Comfortable service level
- <2: Debt service stress risk
- <1: Distress signal

**Debt Service Coverage Ratio:**
- Operating Cash Flow / Total Debt Service
- Cash generation ability
- >1.5: Good cash generation
- <1: Cannot service debt from operations

### Efficiency Ratios

**Asset Turnover:**
- Revenue / Average Total Assets
- How efficiently assets generate sales
- Higher: More efficient
- Varies by industry (retail vs capital intensive)

**Inventory Turnover:**
- COGS / Average Inventory
- How quickly inventory sells
- Higher: Faster conversion to sales
- Low: Slow-moving inventory risk

**Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO):**
- 365 / Inventory Turnover
- Average days inventory held
- High: Cash tied up in inventory
- Low: Quick inventory conversion

**Receivables Turnover:**
- Revenue / Average Receivables
- How quickly receivables collected
- Higher: Better cash collection
- Low: Credit policy too lenient

**Days Sales Outstanding (DSO):**
- 365 / Receivables Turnover
- Average days to collect payment
- Industry specific: 30-90 days typical
- Rising DSO: Collection issues

### Growth Ratios

**Revenue Growth:**
- (Current Year Revenue - Prior Year) / Prior Year Revenue
- YoY growth rate
- Should exceed inflation rate
- Consistent growth: Positive indicator

**Earnings Per Share (EPS) Growth:**
- (Current EPS - Prior EPS) / Prior EPS
- Shareholder value growth
- Compare to dividend yield
- Watch for dilution from new shares

**Book Value Growth:**
- Growth in shareholder equity
- Reflects retained earnings impact
- Combined with ROE: Total return analysis

## Valuation Metrics

### Earnings-Based Metrics

**Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E):**
- Stock Price / EPS
- How much paid per rupee of earnings
- Lower P/E: Cheaper (or slower growth expected)
- Higher P/E: Premium (or higher growth expected)
- Cyclically adjusted P/E (CAPE): Adjusts for cycle

**PEG Ratio (Price/Earnings to Growth):**
- P/E Ratio / Expected Growth Rate
- Compares valuation to growth
- Ratio <1: Undervalued relative to growth
- Ratio >1: Overvalued relative to growth

**Earnings Yield:**
- EPS / Stock Price = 1 / P/E
- Expected return if perpetual earnings
- Compare to bond yields
- Higher yield: Potentially attractive

### Asset-Based Metrics

**Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B):**
- Market Cap / Book Value of Equity
- Tangible asset backing
- <1: Trading below book value (value indicator)
- >3: Premium valuation (growth stock)
- Less relevant for service companies

**Asset Coverage Ratio:**
- (Assets - Liabilities) / Debt
- Asset backing for debt
- >2: Conservative debt level
- <1: Assets less than debt (distress)

### Cash Flow Metrics

**Price-to-Cash Flow:**
- Stock Price / Operating Cash Flow Per Share
- Based on cash generation
- Less manipulable than earnings
- Lower than P/E: Quality indication

**Dividend Yield:**
- Annual Dividend Per Share / Stock Price
- Return from dividends
- 2-4%: Typical mature company
- 5%+: High yield (verify sustainability)

**Free Cash Flow Yield:**
- FCF Per Share / Stock Price
- Cash available for distribution
- Must be positive and growing

## DuPont Analysis

**Three Factor Model:**
1. **Net Profit Margin**: (Profitability)
2. **Asset Turnover**: (Efficiency)
3. **Equity Multiplier**: (Leverage)
- ROE = Margin × Turnover × Multiplier

**Five Factor Model (Extended):**
- Includes tax burden and interest burden
- Analyzes impact of financing decisions
- Helps identify sources of ROE change

## Quality Checks

**Earnings Quality:**
- Compare net income to operating cash flow
- Should be similar (earnings not inflated)
- Growing FCF: Quality earnings
- Declining FCF: Earnings quality concerns

**Dividend Sustainability:**
- Dividend / Operating Cash Flow <50%: Safe
- Dividend / Operating Cash Flow >75%: Risky
- Dividend growth should match earnings growth

**Red Flags:**
- Inventory buildup without sales growth
- Declining operating cash flow
- Rising debt with declining profitability
- Constant equity dilution
- Off-balance sheet financing
- Unusual related party transactions
