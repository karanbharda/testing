# F&O Basics

## Futures & Options Market

The Futures & Options (F&O) segment allows trading in derivative contracts based on underlying assets. NSE IF&O is India's largest derivatives exchange.

## Contract Specifications

### Index Futures
- Underlying: Nifty 50, Bank Nifty, Nifty IT, etc.
- Contract Size: Lot size varies by index
- Expiry: Monthly contracts (last Thursday)
- Trading Hours: 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM IST
- Settlement: Cash settlement on expiry

### Stock Futures
- Underlying: Eligible stocks from F&O list
- Contract Size: Lot size based on stock price
- Expiry: Monthly contracts
- Eligibility: Stocks in top 500 by market cap, 80% trading days with positive net worth

### Index Options
- Call Options: Right to buy at strike price
- Put Options: Right to sell at strike price
- Strike Prices: At-the-money ± multiples of 50-100 points
- Premium: Time value + intrinsic value
- Exercise: European style (only on expiry)

### Stock Options
- Similar to index options
- American style exercise (anytime before expiry)
- Minimum contract size: Market lot

## Margin Requirements

### SPAN Margin
- Standard Portfolio Analysis of Risk
- Calculated for each position
- Considers worst-case loss scenarios
- Updated daily at 6:00 PM

### Exposure Margin
- Additional margin for concentrated positions
- 3-5% of contract value for indices
- Higher for stocks (5-10%)

### Mark-to-Market (MTM)
- Daily settlement of profits/losses
- Prevents accumulation of losses
- Mandatory margin top-up if losses exceed available funds

## Position Limits

### Client Level
- Index futures: 20% of market-wide open interest
- Stock futures: 20% of market-wide open interest
- Index options: No specific limit
- Stock options: 1% of free float or ₹500 crore, whichever lower

### Market-wide Limits
- Index futures: 95% of previous day's open interest
- Stock derivatives: 95% of average daily turnover

## F&O Ban Mechanism

### SEBI's F&O Ban Rules
- Trigger: When stock moves ±20% in a day
- Duration: Minimum 5 trading days
- Criteria: Price movement + volume surge
- Review: Daily basis for removal

### Ban Conditions
1. Price movement: ≥20% from previous close
2. Volume: ≥500% of 60-day average
3. Delivery: <50% of traded quantity
4. Combined criteria for ban imposition

### Trading Restrictions
- No fresh positions allowed
- Existing positions can be closed
- No exercise of options during ban
- Automatic removal after 5 days if conditions normalize

## Risk Management
- Daily margin collection
- Position monitoring
- Surveillance systems
- Circuit breakers at ±10%, ±15%, ±20%
- Special margins during high volatility
